## Brand Identity

**Product name:** FlowPilot AI Lead Concierge
**Tagline:** Stop letting warm leads go cold.
**Positioning statement:** An AI lead response system with operational memory — designed to qualify leads, capture next actions, and improve consistency over time.

- Premium but not corporate — feels like a smart local operator
- Human and practical — no buzzwords, no hype
- Suitable for Australian small businesses (tradies, dealerships, local services)

- Body text: `#CBD5E1`

- Muted text: `#64748B`

### Typography (suggest to Claude Code)
- Display/headings: `Inter` or `Plus Jakarta Sans` — weight 700–800, tight tracking
- Body: `Inter` — weight 400–500, generous line height
- Mono/data labels: `JetBrains Mono` — for step numbers, tags, diagnostic references

## Visual Design Direction

Dark, calm, operationally intelligent. Not sci-fi. Think: a well-run control room at 11pm, everything working quietly in the background.

**Signature element:** A subtle animated "pulse" on the hero — like a radar sweep or signal ping — that communicates "FlowPilot is monitoring, responding, working." This one ambient motion underpins the whole "after-hours AI" concept.

**Cards:** Subtle glassmorphism on dark surfaces — `rgba(255,255,255,0.04)` background, `1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.08)` border, `backdrop-filter: blur(8px)` — but used sparingly. Not decorative.

**Animations:** Scroll-triggered fade-in for sections. No excessive motion. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. The pulse in the hero is the only ambient animation.

### SECTION 5: HOW IT WORKS

**Section eyebrow:** How It Works

**Headline:**
> Six steps. All automatic.

**Steps:**

1. **Lead comes in**
   A new enquiry arrives from your website, Facebook lead form, or Google ad.

2. **FlowPilot follows up**
   The AI sends an immediate SMS or email response — or places a voice call — to engage the lead while they're still warm.

3. **Lead is qualified**
   FlowPilot asks the right questions: what they need, their timeline, urgency, and any key objections.

4. **Appointment or callback is captured**
   If the lead is ready, FlowPilot books a time or requests a callback and confirms with the customer.

5. **CRM or sheet is updated**
   Lead details, qualification notes, outcome, and next action are logged to your CRM, Airtable, or Google Sheet automatically.

6. **Your team receives a summary**
   A clean handover — lead name, what they said, what they need, and what to do next — is sent to the right person on your team.

### SECTION 11: FAQ

**Section eyebrow:** Questions

**Headline:**
> Common questions answered.

---

**Does this replace my team?**
No. FlowPilot handles the first-response and qualification step so your team focuses on conversations that are ready to move forward. Your team always receives a clear handover summary and remains in control of the customer relationship.

**What is Hermes?**
Hermes is FlowPilot's AI reasoning and operational memory layer. It helps interpret lead data, apply your business rules consistently, generate handover summaries, and flag recurring workflow issues. Think of it as operational context that persists across your workflows — not a separate AI product.

**What does FlowPilot remember?**
It can retain operational context including approved scripts, qualification rules, appointment logic, escalation preferences, common customer objections, and recurring workflow fixes. What is retained and how it's handled can be scoped during onboarding.

**Can I approve the AI script first?**
Yes. All scripts, qualification questions, and handover formats are reviewed and approved by you before the system goes live. Nothing runs without your sign-off.

**Can it work with my current CRM?**
FlowPilot can connect to Airtable, Google Sheets, and common CRMs. If you're using a specific platform, we'll scope the integration during onboarding. Complex CRM integrations may require additional setup time.

**Can it use SMS?**
Yes. SMS follow-up is included via Twilio integration. Email notifications are also included. Voice calling (AI voice agent) is available depending on your use case.

**What happens if the AI can't answer?**
If FlowPilot can't confidently handle a response, it flags the lead for human follow-up and sends your team a notification. The system is designed to hand off, not guess.

**How long does setup take?**
Most setups are completed within 1–2 weeks from the scoping session. This includes workflow design, integration, testing, and onboarding.

**Is this suitable if I only get a few leads?**
If you're receiving fewer than 5–10 leads per month, the ROI of an automated system may not be there yet. We'll discuss your lead volume during the discovery call and be honest about whether it's a good fit.

**Do I need technical knowledge?**
No. FlowPilot is a done-for-you service. You don't need to build or configure anything yourself. You'll be walked through how it works and what to expect, but the technical setup is handled entirely by us.

**Does the system improve automatically?**
FlowPilot supports a human-reviewed improvement loop. The system flags outcomes, identifies patterns, and surfaces suggested refinements — but changes to scripts, rules, and workflows are reviewed and approved before being applied. It does not update itself without oversight.

**Can I update or change the AI's business rules?**
Yes. During the pilot, business rules, qualification criteria, and scripts can be updated at any time. This is a collaborative process — your feedback is how the system gets better.

**Can I review what the AI is using to make decisions?**
Yes. Operational memory, scripts, and qualification rules are visible and reviewable. During the pilot, we'll walk through what the system is using and why, and refine it based on real outcomes.

## Implementation Notes for Claude Code

### Stack
- React + Tailwind CSS
- Single-page application (all sections on one page)
- Smooth scroll navigation
- Accordion component for FAQ
- Scroll-triggered fade-in for sections (Intersection Observer)

### Key Components
1. `<Navbar />` — sticky, transparent on hero, dark on scroll
2. `<Hero />` — ambient pulse animation (CSS radial gradient, keyframe opacity)
3. `<Problem />` — 3-card grid
4. `<Solution />` — prose + Hermes panel (distinct bordered card)
5. `<HowItWorks />` — horizontal step flow (desktop), vertical (mobile)
6. `<WhatsIncluded />` — 2-col checklist grid
7. `<HermesSection />` — 3-card feature grid + note
8. `<UseCases />` — 2x2 card grid
9. `<Pricing />` — 2-column pricing cards + amber urgency banner
10. `<EarlyAdvantage />` — 4-point horizontal list
11. `<FAQ />` — accordion (all 13 questions)
12. `<FinalCTA />` — centred, dark section
13. `<Footer />` — 3-column

### Animations
- Hero: one ambient CSS `@keyframes` pulse on a `radial-gradient` circle — opacity 0.3 → 0.07 → 0.3, 4s ease-in-out infinite
- All sections: `opacity: 0; transform: translateY(20px)` → visible on Intersection Observer trigger
- Honour `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`

### CTA Button Styles

- Secondary: `text-slate-300 underline underline-offset-4 hover:text-white transition`
